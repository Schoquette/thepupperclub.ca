Your Questions,
Answered

We primarily focus on dog walking, or "visits". We come to your home, take your pup out, and provide any other agreed upon care.

Dog-sitting can be discussed, or we can make recommendations if we are unable to accommodate.

Our business is founded on building relationships and getting to know both you and your pup(s) as our clients. We can offer the personalized service we do by having a limited roster of clients, and consistent interactions.

We want to make sure we are always able to accommodate these visits, without one-off requests.

We allow up to four weeks per year of pausing. Any months with paused weeks will have a pro-rated discount, or be credited to you to apply to a future month.

For longer interruptions such as snowbird lifestyles, we are happy to discuss how this will be accommodated on a case-by-case basis.

This is up to you!

You are entitled to solo walks, however many dogs enjoy and benefit from socialization with other dogs.

This will be discussed during your intake, and any dogs that are paired up will be carefully selected.

We primarily service Port Moody and Anmore. Certain areas in Coquitlam and Belcarra can be accommodated.

Collaborative, honest, and straightforward. We're here to guide the process, bring ideas to the table, and keep things moving.

We are breed-agnostic. Since the care is so customized, we are able to adjust the activities within the visit to accommodate different walk lengths or energy output.

There are no blanket behaviours we will not work with. If safety becomes a concern, we will help you find a trainer.

As part of the intake process, please share with us any training you are working on, so that we can help reinforce good behaviours, and discourage bad ones.

We use a tool called TimeToPet. We will set up a recurring schedule for consistency, and you will be able to request changes as needed through the platform.

We take pride in having an accident-free history., but we don't let that get to our heads.

A few of the things we do:

  • Tractive tracking collars are placed on all dogs during walks
  • If being transported by car, we secure them by seatbelt or gate depending on their size
  • We confirm with you any allergies or dietary restrictions, and never feed them anything outside of this
  • We will collect veterinary information from you, and always know the nearest emergency vet
  • Handlers are certified in Pet First Aid, delivered by the Red Cross
  • We have current and comprehensive insurance
  • We use our heads, and always communicate with you if we are uncertain about any instructions or limitations
