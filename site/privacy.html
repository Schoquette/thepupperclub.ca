Privacy Policy

How we collect, use, and protect your personal information.

Last updated: April 22, 2026

The Pupper Club ("we," "us," or "our") is committed to protecting your personal information. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard information in accordance with British Columbia's Personal Information Protection Act (PIPA) and applicable federal privacy legislation.

This policy applies to our public website at thepupperclub.ca and our client portal. The information we collect differs depending on how you interact with us.

2. Information We Collect

A. Public Website Visitors

When you visit our public website, we collect minimal information:

  • Contact form submissions: Your name, email address, phone number, and message when you voluntarily submit our contact form.
  • Cookies: We use only functional cookies necessary for the website to operate. We do not use advertising or tracking cookies.

B. Registered Clients (Client Portal)

When you become a client, we collect additional information necessary to provide our dog care services:

  • Account information: Name, email address, phone number, home address, and emergency contact details.
  • Home access information: Entry codes, key locations, or other instructions needed to access your home for visits. This information is encrypted at rest.
  • Pet information: Dog names, breeds, ages, health conditions, behavioural notes, veterinarian contacts, and vaccination records.
  • Photos and images: Photos taken during visits for report cards and communication, as well as profile photos you upload.
  • Communications: Messages exchanged through the client portal, email, and SMS.
  • Payment information: Billing details processed through Stripe. We do not store your full credit card number on our servers.
  • Service records: Appointment history, visit reports, invoices, and related service documentation.
  • Location data: Mileage and distance information related to service visits, calculated via Google Maps.

3. How We Use Your Information

We use your personal information for the following purposes:

  • Providing and managing our dog walking and pet care services
  • Communicating with you about appointments, visit reports, and service updates
  • Processing payments and managing invoices
  • Accessing your home to provide in-home pet care services
  • Ensuring the health and safety of dogs in our care
  • Responding to inquiries submitted through our contact form
  • Sending service-related notifications (appointment reminders, report cards)
  • Sending marketing communications (only with your express consent)
  • Calculating mileage for service delivery
  • Maintaining records for tax and legal compliance

4. Consent

We obtain your consent before collecting, using, or disclosing your personal information:

  • Express consent is obtained for sensitive information such as home access codes, health records, payment details, and marketing communications.
  • Implied consent may be relied upon when the purpose of collection is obvious from the circumstances, such as providing your address so we can walk your dog.

You may withdraw your consent at any time by contacting us. We will inform you of any consequences of withdrawing consent, such as our inability to continue providing certain services.

5. Third-Party Service Providers

We use the following third-party services to operate our business. Your personal information may be processed by these providers, some of which are based in the United States:

  • Stripe (United States) — Payment processing. Subject to Stripe's Privacy Policy.
  • Resend (United States) — Email delivery for service notifications.
  • Google Maps (United States) — Address lookup and mileage calculation.
  • GoDaddy (United States) — Website and application hosting.

Cross-border transfers: As required by BC PIPA, we inform you that your personal information may be stored or accessed in the United States through these service providers. These providers are bound by their own privacy policies and data protection commitments.

6. Safeguards

We protect your personal information using security measures appropriate to the sensitivity of the information:

  • All data transmitted between your browser and our servers is encrypted using HTTPS/TLS.
  • Home access codes are encrypted at rest using industry-standard encryption.
  • Payment information is handled by Stripe and is never stored on our servers.
  • Access to personal information is restricted to authorized personnel only.
  • Client portal accounts are protected by passwords and authentication tokens.

7. Data Retention

We retain personal information only as long as necessary to fulfill the purposes for which it was collected, or as required by law:

  • Financial records (invoices, payment history): 7 years, as required by the Canada Revenue Agency.
  • Client account information: For the duration of the client relationship, plus up to 2 years afterward.
  • Home access codes: Deleted promptly when services are terminated.
  • Visit photos and report cards: Retained during the client relationship and for a reasonable period afterward.
  • Communications: Retained during the client relationship, deleted within 1 year of service ending.
  • Contact form submissions: Retained until the inquiry is resolved, then deleted within 1 year.

8. Your Rights

Under BC PIPA, you have the right to:

  • Access the personal information we hold about you. We will respond to access requests within 30 business days.
  • Request correction of any inaccurate or incomplete personal information.
  • Withdraw consent for the collection, use, or disclosure of your personal information, subject to legal or contractual limitations.
  • Request deletion of your personal information where no legal retention obligation exists.

To exercise any of these rights, contact our Privacy Officer using the information in Section 1.

9. Cookies

Our website uses only functional cookies necessary for authentication and session management in the client portal. We do not use advertising, analytics, or tracking cookies. No cookie consent banner is required for functional cookies under Canadian law.

10. Marketing Communications

In compliance with Canada's Anti-Spam Legislation (CASL), we only send marketing communications with your express consent. Every marketing message includes an unsubscribe option. Unsubscribe requests are honoured within 10 business days. Service-related messages (appointment confirmations, visit reports, invoices) are not considered marketing and may be sent without separate consent.

11. Complaints

If you have concerns about how we handle your personal information, please contact our Privacy Officer first. If you are not satisfied with our response, you may file a complaint with:

12. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. We will notify registered clients of material changes via email or through the client portal. The "Last updated" date at the top of this page indicates the most recent revision.
