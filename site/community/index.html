How it works

Intentionally simple.

01 — Verify

Real people, nearby.

Every member completes ID and selfie verification, and their address is tied to a neighbourhood radius — without ever being shown to anyone else.

02 — Connect

Build a small circle.

Browse verified neighbours in your neighbourhood. Send a thoughtful note. Build a private network of people you trust, on your own terms.

03 — Coordinate

Quietly ask for help.

When life happens, send a small broadcast to chosen neighbours. The first to confirm opens a private chat. No pressure, no public posts, no obligation.

Privacy as a feature, not a setting.

The Community is built on a few non-negotiable principles. These are the foundation, not features to be revisited.

  • Addresses are never shared. Not in profiles, not in messages, not in any export. Distance shows as “Less than 500m away” — never an address, never a pin on a map.
  • Verification is required before any interaction. No browsing other members, no messaging, no broadcasts until verified.
  • No public profiles, no feeds, no comments. The Community is not a social network. There is no surface for public-facing content — and therefore no harassment vector that resembles one.
  • No ratings, no scores, no negative reviews. Members may leave positive written recommendations. Care is never reduced to a number, and concerns about a member go through a private reporting flow.
  • No money. No payments, no tipping, no premium tier. The Community is freely given and freely received.
  • Meet in public the first time. A gentle, persistent reminder — a park, a café, a building lobby — for first meetings between neighbours.
